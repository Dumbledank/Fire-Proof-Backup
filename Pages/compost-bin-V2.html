Compost Bin V2

The rotation...

Idea

The initial motivation for this project was to design and eventually build a compost bin for my parents' garden. Behind this motivation lay an appreciation of the importance of composting as a natural process.

I view composting as the mechanism by which the old and dead may nourish the new and living. A banana peel will decompose, be absorbed into the earth and nourish other plant life, thus continuing the rotation of decay and regeneration.

The structure we see here is a way of establishing order around a natural process. It exists to meet a desire to have rotting waste contained, out of sight and out of mind.

However, it goes without saying, a simple enclosure such as this does not strictly improve the quality and composition of the organic content contained within it. The compost would mature just as well, if not marginally better, as an open heap on the ground. And in a simpler time, our ancestors would, most likely, never have had a designated spot to put their waste, it would just go anywhere on the ground.

Nevertheless, in modern society, with limitations on the size of our outdoor spaces, there is benefit in having structures such as this in the garden. Most notably it gives gardeners a place to store green waste which will eventually degrade into a suitably rich soil for plants to thrive in.
