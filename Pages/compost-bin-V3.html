Idea

This design is a very similar to the previous version but slightly modified. I wanted the components to interlock in a more stable configuration pattern. Hence within each layer, each component overlaps the next.

For this prototype version and the one previous, I knew I wanted to have the components tessellate with each other when they lie flat on a sheet of material. This was predominantly born out of a desire to be economical with my use of material. However, after some time spent engaging with this idea on paper, I soon discovered that this design constraint was an interesting one to explore from an aesthetic standpoint.

I should mention that although I had a similar much less refined idea earlier on, before this version, I stumbled upon two sources of interest which persuaded me to explore the idea with more gusto. The first was a book on Celtic key patterns by Ian Bain, and the second source was from a talk given by Edward de Bono on lateral thinking.
